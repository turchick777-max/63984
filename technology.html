01

Process Overview

AQUINTAQA is based on a volumetric gas–sorbent separation mechanism designed to remove dissolved ions from saline water through controlled physicochemical interaction within a multiphase system.

The process differs fundamentally from pressure-driven membrane desalination and thermal evaporation technologies. Instead of forcing water through a selective barrier or applying phase-change energy, the system utilizes:

  • Controlled gas-phase interaction
  • Selective sorbent-mediated ion capture
  • Directional mass transfer within a volumetric reactor environment

The separation occurs within a dynamically regulated chamber designed to optimize ion selectivity, process stability, and energy balance.

The platform is modular and configurable depending on feed salinity and operating conditions.

02

High-Level Architecture

The system architecture consists of:

  1. Feed conditioning module
  2. Volumetric reaction chamber
  3. Gas-phase circulation and control unit
  4. Sorbent regeneration subsystem
  5. Separation and recovery module
  6. Process control and monitoring system

The architecture is designed for:

  • Multi-feed adaptability
  • Stable long-duration operation
  • Controlled mass transfer environment
  • Industrial scalability

No high-pressure membrane stacks are used.
No high-temperature evaporation stages are required.

03

Operating Envelope

Validated operating ranges (pilot level):

  • Feed salinity range: 15–210 g/L
  • Continuous runs: ≥300 hours
  • Multi-feed operation confirmed
  • Baseline directional ion reduction demonstrated

Testing includes controlled laboratory validation before and after treatment cycles (independent laboratory verification under NDA).

04

Comparison with Conventional Desalination

Reverse Osmosis (RO)

  • Pressure-driven membrane process
  • Membrane fouling and scaling risks
  • High-pressure pump dependency
  • Sensitivity to feed composition

Thermal Distillation

  • Energy-intensive phase change
  • High CAPEX infrastructure
  • Large-scale fixed installations

AQUINTAQA Platform

  • No membrane-based separation
  • No high-pressure requirement
  • No bulk phase-change energy input
  • Volumetric ion-selective mechanism
  • Modular reactor configuration
The differentiation is structural, not incremental.
