01

Quality Philosophy

AQUINTAQA is developed as a controlled engineering system rather than an experimental laboratory concept.

The process is governed by defined operating parameters, traceable testing cycles, and structured documentation practices.

Quality is approached as a system property, not a performance claim. Every configuration is evaluated within defined feed ranges, monitored operating envelopes, and repeatable procedures. Process stability, ion reduction directionality, and recovery ratios are assessed under documented baseline conditions.

The objective is not only to demonstrate technical feasibility, but to ensure that the system behaves predictably across validated feed compositions and operating configurations. All technical iterations are logged, version-controlled, and reviewed under internal governance procedures.

02

Testing Methodology

Validation activities are conducted using controlled sample preparation and documented baseline measurements prior to each run.

Testing framework includes:

  • Defined feed salinity and composition parameters
  • Controlled reactor configuration and gas–sorbent dosing conditions
  • Continuous monitoring of process stability
  • Structured before/after sampling

Each testing cycle begins with baseline characterization of the incoming feed (TDS, ion composition, and key parameters). Following controlled operation under documented settings, post-treatment samples are collected and prepared according to traceable procedures.

03

Independent Validation

External laboratory involvement forms a core component of the validation process.

Independent testing covers:

  • Pre- and post-treatment analytical verification
  • Chemical parameter confirmation
  • Laboratory measurement protocols aligned with recognized analytical standards

All laboratory interactions are documented. Sample origin, preparation method, and handling procedures are recorded to maintain data integrity.

Disclosure framework:

  • Public site: high-level validation status only
  • Post e-NDA: laboratory reports, analytical methods, extended datasets

No certification or regulatory approval claims are made on this page. Validation status reflects structured technical testing under defined conditions.

04

Validation Focus Areas

Independent laboratories conduct analytical measurements for before/after comparisons under chain-of-custody handling where applicable. Repeatability is evaluated through multi-run testing across varying feed salinity ranges.

Validation focus areas include:

  • Directional ion reduction consistency
  • Product water recovery behavior
  • Concentrate management profile
  • Operational stability across extended runs (≥300 hours cumulative pilot operation)

Testing results are compiled into structured reports. Public disclosures present only high-level status indicators. Detailed numerical datasets, analytical methods, and extended testing logs are available under e-NDA within the Data Room.

06

Quality System & Governance

AQUINTAQA operates under structured internal quality governance procedures.

Core elements include:

  • Document version control
  • Controlled configuration tracking
  • Testing protocol standardization
  • Process parameter logging
  • Data traceability framework

Each prototype and pilot configuration is documented with revision history and operating context. Testing protocols define feed parameters, run conditions, sampling intervals, and logging requirements.

Operational data are archived and linked to configuration versions to maintain traceability across development stages.

Where applicable, future industrial implementation will align with formal quality management standards appropriate to jurisdiction and deployment context.
